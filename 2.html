Symptoms

Symptoms in adults

  • Rashes commonly appear in creases of the elbows or knees or the nape of the neck.
  • Rashes cover much of the body.
  • Rashes can be especially prominent on the neck, face, and around the eyes.
  • Rashes can cause very dry skin.
  • Rashes can be permanently itchy.
  • Rashes in adults can be more scaly than those occurring in children.
  • Rashes can lead to skin infections.

Adults who developed atopic dermatitis as a child but no longer experience the condition may still have dry or easily-irritated skin, hand eczema, and eye problems. The appearance of skin affected by atopic dermatitis will depend on how much a person scratches and whether the skin is infected.
Scratching and rubbing further irritate the skin, increase inflammation, and make itchiness worse.
